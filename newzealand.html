Study In Newzealand

Get a world class qualification from the beautiful Island Country in Oceania. New Zealand is known for its natural beauty and diverse culture. It offers a wide range of courses in comparatively affordable University fees and living expenses. The best thing about education in New Zealand is that their Degrees/Universities are internationally recognized.

  • New Zealand government has designed their education system with a goal to bring in 95% international student satisfaction rate by 2025 and 97% by 2035.
  • New Zealand Government offers numerous scholarships for International students.
  • New Zealand qualifications are recognized by employers worldwide.
  • International students are entitled to the same legal rights as normal citizens of New Zealand.
  • New Zealand is one of the safest countries to reside. New Zealand is ranks eighth among the world’s happiest countries.
  • Students are allowed to work part-time while studying in New Zealand. This will help you learn the work culture of the place as well earn for your expenses.

  • New Zealand has eight state-funded universities and 18 Institutes of Technology and Polytechnics. In addition to this it has a lot of Private Training Establishments. The top eight universities of New Zealand are Auckland University of Technology, University of Canterbury, Lincoln University, University of Otago, University of Auckland, Massey University, University of Waikato and Victoria University of Wellington.
  • All the eight state-funded New Zealand universities are ranked amongst the world’s best universities by QS World University Rankings.Education in New Zealand is highly practical oriented. Their Universities are Government regulated and best funded.

  • Polytechnic Diploma Courses range from NZ$13,500 – NZ$16,000 per year depending on the course
  • Universities Degree range from NZ$15,000 – NZ$18,000 per year for most under-graduate degrees
  • Post-graduate Degrees begin from NZ$16,000 per year and MBA Degrees are about NZ$22,000

* Tuition fees vary depending on the institution and the study programme

Applying & Visa Procedure

How to Apply

Intakes : February / July /October

To be able to study in New Zealand, you need to be aware of the academic year, which is from mid / late February to early November. Most Universities have 2 semesters with a holiday of 4 weeks in June. Each University operates on slightly different dates and some also offer summer school in the main holiday period from November to February. Intakes are in February, July and sometimes in September.

Basic Requirements:- You must be able to show that you are :

  • Genuine Student
  • Going to follow a recognized full-time course
  • Be able to pay for your course and support yourself
  • Be able to intend to follow your chosen course and
  • Intend to leave the country at the end of the period authorized for your stay
